some operator properties

categories: physics

Jan 5, 2020

Required level: Quantum Mechanics.

commuting exponentials of operators

For any operator $\hat{O}$, let's define its time-dependent version, $\hat{O}_t$ to be

$$ \hat{O}_t= e^{+i \hat{H} t} \hat{O} e^{-i \hat{H} t}. $$

From this definition, it is easily shown that

$$ \frac{d \hat{O}_t}{dt}=+i\left[\hat{H},\hat{O}_t\right]=+i\left[\hat{H},\hat{O}\right], $$

where the last equality is since $e^{\pm i \hat{H}t}$ commutes with $\hat{H}$. Let's consider the case where

$$ \left[\hat{H},\hat{O}_t\right]=\hat{C} \hat{O}_t. $$

Then, we can set up the differential equation for $\hat{O}_t$ as

$$ \frac{d \hat{O}_t}{dt}=+i\hat{C} \hat{O}_t \implies \hat{O}_t = e^{+i\hat{C}t} \hat{O}. $$

Substituting the definition of $\hat{O}_t$, we can find the `commutation' rule:

$$ \forall ; \hat{O} \text{ such that } \left[\hat{H},\hat{O}\right]=\hat{C} \hat{O}, $$

$$ \hat{O} e^{\pm i \hat{H} t} = e^{\pm i \hat{H} t} e^{\mp i \hat{C} t} \hat{O}, $$

$$ e^{\pm i \hat{H} t} \hat{O}= e^{ \pm i \hat{C} t} \hat{O} e^{ \pm i \hat{H} t}. $$

Similarly, for the case where

$$ \forall ; \hat{O} \text{ such that } \left[\hat{H},\hat{O}\right]= \hat{O} \hat{C}, $$

$$ \hat{O} e^{\pm i \hat{H} t}= e^{\pm i \hat{H} t} \hat{O} e^{\mp i \hat{C} t}, $$

$$ e^{\pm i \hat{H} t} \hat{O}=\hat{O} e^{\pm i \hat{C} t} e^{\pm i \hat{H} t}. $$

commuting creation/annhilation operators with number operators

Consider annhilation operator $\hat{\eta}_a$, and number operator $\hat{I}_a$.

To quadratic order:

$$ \left[ \hat{\eta}_{a}, \hat{I}_{b} \right] = \delta_{a,b}\hat{\eta}_{a} $$

and

$$ \left[ \hat{\eta}^\dagger_a, \hat{I}_b \right] = - \hat{\eta}^\dagger_a \delta_{a,b}. $$

For quartic order:

$$ \left[ \hat{\eta}_a, \hat{I}_b \hat{I}_c \right] = \left(\delta_{a,b}\delta_{b,c} + \delta_{a,b} \hat{I}_c + \delta_{a,c} \hat{I}_b \right) \hat{\eta}_a $$

and

$$ \left[ \hat{\eta}^\dagger_a, \hat{I}_b \hat{I}_c \right] = - \hat{\eta}^\dagger_a \left(\delta_{a,b}\delta_{b,c} + \delta_{a,b} \hat{I}_c + \delta_{a,c} \hat{I}_b \right). $$
